Where have all the Artists gone?

Our focus for this project: What issues the art Community we're facing And did aspiring artists or artists looking to advance their skills have trouble finding a mentor or a learning platform to their liking.

We had 4 weeks to carry out our research and creating a prototype. As the project manager, I assess what are deliverables were for the week and broke down tasks into manageable pieces.

What is an Artist?

First, we tried to get into the headspace of our users by each of us coming up with an archetype and a persona. What challenges were they facing? Why would they be looking to improve their art? Why might they be wanting to teach art? Art being very Diverse in mediums, we decided to focus more so on traditional art, painting and drawing mostly for this exercise.

Half of our group, there were 4 of us, actually had previous art experience. I myself at the time wanted to learn more about gouache. What we had come up with was students, like me, who wanted to learn a new art medium or wanted resources to learn from and teachers who wanted to pass on their knowledge or wanted to share their love of art with the community.

Our next task was to find out if our thoughts were correct or if they were way out in left field. So we conducted several interviews, with art students and established artists, and made a Google survey to see what artists we were looking to learn, and where they mostly get their information from.

What we found in our interviews was kind of surprising, most artists are both the teacher and the student. In that that those who know a lot about their art, and teach it, seek to learn more or experience it. Even those Who are starting out would share resources or what they've learned to those around them. Our survey showed that a vast majority of people used YouTube to learn art Yet a high number of people preferred workshop environments. The most sought-after medium to learn was painting and drawing.

What do artist want

From here we synthesized our data into post notes on Miro and Organize them into pain points, types of learning, and what was important to the artist. We had a lot that convey that peer review and feedback were very important for them as well as freedom of expression and ease of access. [insted how might we slide]

We found our goal! Through our findings, we discovered how important validation is to an artist that they are improving in their art and that they have a community that encourages freedom of expression. To see how we could turn this into a platform/app/website we made a Created Matrix in Miro. Noting all the different necessities an artist would be looking for or inquiring about Depending on where they are on their Journey. Along the way, we discovered that our Matrix actually represented the growth of an artist and a potential business model for a website.
